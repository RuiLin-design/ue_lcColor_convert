Bridging the color gap between design mockups and the game engine.

Our Technical Story

Hello! We are a group of Technical Artists and C++ UI Programmers who are deeply passionate about game development. While working on various AAA and indie titles, we repeatedly hit a frustrating workflow bottleneck: color distortion across different software.

When UI/UX designers hand off pixel-perfect designs from Figma, Photoshop, or Sketch, the nightmare usually begins. Directly plugging those HEX or sRGB values into Unreal Engine 5's UMG widgets or Material Blueprints results in colors that look washed out, desaturated, or muddy in-engine.

Initially, teams often rely on manual visual tweaking, which not only wastes valuable development time but also sparks endless "it doesn't look like the mockup" debates between the art and engineering departments.

The Core Pain Point: Why Is This Tool Necessary?

To solve this at its root, we have to look at Unreal Engine's rendering pipeline. The answer is simple but critical: modern engines rely on Physically Based Rendering (PBR). To ensure absolute physical accuracy in lighting and shadow calculations, all internal color math in UE5 must happen in Linear Color Space.

However, monitors and most design software use the sRGB color space with a Gamma 2.2 curve to match human non-linear visual perception. Feeding sRGB data directly into a linear renderer without strict mathematical inverse gamma correction is a recipe for disaster.

We tried various online calculators and Excel spreadsheets, but they either lacked precision (causing subtle color shifts) or were too clunky for high-frequency UI development. Thus, the UE5 Color Converter was born—designed to be the ultimate, always-open side-monitor companion.

Our Philosophy & Promises

Absolutely Free

We know the struggles of indie devs and small studios. The core conversion features will remain permanently free with no hidden subscriptions, serving as our contribution to the global UE community.

Privacy First

Whether it's a confidential project palette or a quick test color, all math is executed locally in your browser via JavaScript. Zero data is ever uploaded to our servers.

Uncompromising Precision

We strictly follow the standard sRGB to Linear piecewise function, ensuring the generated FLinearColor floats are accurate to six decimal places to satisfy the engine's underlying renderer.

Developer-Centric

Toggle HEX formats or copy C++ struct parameters in one click. We believe in subtraction—removing all unnecessary clutter that gets in the way of your efficiency.
